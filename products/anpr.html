Overview

What it does

Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) reads vehicle plates directly from live camera feeds and matches them against authority-held watchlists — flagging vehicles linked to criminal activity and enabling a vehicle to be tracked as it moves across the camera network.

ANPR complements facial recognition on the AMS platform: where one identifies people of interest, the other identifies and follows vehicles of interest, with every read logged and timestamped for investigation.

How it works

Capture → detect → act

STEP 01

Read

The model captures and decodes plates from live traffic feeds.

STEP 02

Match

Each plate is checked against the authority's vehicle watchlist.

STEP 03

Track

Flagged vehicles are logged and followed across cameras on the dashboard.

Use cases

Where it's deployed

  • Vehicle interdiction. Flag plates linked to criminal activity in real time.
  • Checkpoints. Read and verify plates at entry and control points.
  • Investigations. Reconstruct a vehicle's movement from logged reads.
  • Access control. Manage authorised-vehicle entry to secure zones.
