Facial Recognition

Match the face. Confirm the threat. In real time.

Overview

What it does

Facial Recognition compares faces detected across live camera feeds against authorised databases of wanted persons, missing individuals, and unauthorised personnel — returning a confidence-scored match in real time so operators can act on verified intelligence rather than hunches.

Watchlist databases are populated solely with data provided by, and under the legal custodianship of, the relevant government authority. Matches are surfaced for human review; the system never makes an autonomous enforcement decision.

How it works

Capture → detect → act

STEP 01

Enrol

The authority loads its legally-held watchlist into the on-premise database.

STEP 02

Match

Faces in live feeds are compared against the watchlist with a confidence score per candidate.

STEP 03

Review

Scored matches reach the dashboard for an authorised operator to confirm and action.

Use cases

Where it's deployed

  • Wanted-person interdiction. Locate suspects on active watchlists across the camera network.
  • Missing persons. Surface a missing child or adult the moment they appear on any feed.
  • Restricted facilities. Flag unauthorised personnel in secure or controlled-access zones.
  • Event security. Screen large public gatherings against threat watchlists.
